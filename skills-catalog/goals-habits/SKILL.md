# Goals & Habits

Turn intentions into tracked progress. Anchor everything to what the user
actually wants (read `Profile.md` first) — goals that aren't theirs don't stick.

## Goals (`Areas/Personal/Goals.md`)

For each goal capture: the **why**, a clear **target**, a **timeline**, and
**milestones**. Make them concrete and measurable where possible.

- **Set:** clarify the goal and its why; break it into milestones; spin the first
  milestone into a project/tasks (task-manager).
- **Review:** show progress vs target, what moved, what stalled, and the next
  milestone. Be honest — celebrate real progress, flag drift without guilt-trips.
- Tie goals to areas (work / freelance / finances / personal) so they're balanced.

## Habits (`Areas/Personal/Habits.md`)

Track recurring habits the user wants to build or keep.

- **Define:** the habit, cadence (daily/weekly), and the cue/why.
- **Check in:** log completions (a simple streak/log); surface today's habits in
  the daily brief.
- **Review:** consistency over the week/month; what's working, what to adjust or
  drop. Favor a few sustained habits over many abandoned ones.

## Visibility

These are most useful when seen daily — daily-brief surfaces today's habit
check-ins and goal-relevant tasks; weekly-review reports progress and resets the
next week. Keep the goal/habit notes current so those skills have real data.

## Principles

- **Realistic over ambitious:** fewer, sustainable commitments beat a long list.
- **Honest tracking:** reflect actual progress; never fabricate streaks or wins.
- **Their goals, their terms** — coach, don't nag.

## What this does NOT do

- It does not invent progress or shame the user for missing.
- It does not set goals the user didn't choose.
